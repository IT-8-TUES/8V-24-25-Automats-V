Stellar Phenomena

The magnificent stars that illuminate our universe and the incredible processes that power them

☀️

The Sun

Our nearest star formed 4.6 billion years ago from a collapsing nebula. Every second, it converts 600 million tons of hydrogen into helium through nuclear fusion, releasing energy equivalent to 100 billion hydrogen bombs.

5,778K Surface Temp
15M K Core Temp
333,000× Earth's Mass
💫

Neutron Stars

The collapsed cores of massive stars, neutron stars are so dense that a teaspoon would weigh about 6 billion tons. They can spin up to 700 times per second and have magnetic fields trillions of times stronger than Earth's.

20km Diameter
1.4M☉ Typical Mass
10¹⁵ G Magnetic Field
💥

Supernovae

When massive stars exhaust their fuel, they explode in supernovae—events so bright they can outshine entire galaxies. These cosmic furnaces forge elements heavier than iron and scatter them across the universe.

10²⁸ Megatons TNT
30 days Peak Brightness
30,000 km/s Ejecta Speed

Planetary Worlds

From rocky terrestrial worlds to gas giants and mysterious dwarf planets

🌍

Earth

Our blue marble harbors the only known life in the universe. Earth's magnetic field, generated by its molten iron core, protects us from deadly solar radiation. The planet's 23.5° axial tilt gives us our seasons.

71% Ocean Coverage
12,756km Diameter
1 AU From Sun
♃

Jupiter

The solar system's giant protector, Jupiter's immense gravity acts as a cosmic vacuum cleaner, deflecting asteroids and comets. Its Great Red Spot is a storm larger than Earth that has raged for centuries.

95 Known Moons
318× Earth's Mass
10h Day Length
🌕

The Moon

Earth's faithful companion stabilizes our planet's rotation and creates tides. Formed from debris after a Mars-sized object collided with early Earth, the Moon is slowly drifting away at 3.8 cm per year.

384,400km Distance
27.3 days Orbital Period
1/6 Earth's Gravity

Galactic Marvels

Vast cosmic structures that organize matter across billions of light-years

🌌

The Milky Way

Our galactic home contains 100-400 billion stars arranged in a barred spiral structure. We orbit the galactic center once every 230 million years at a speed of 828,000 km/h, completing one "galactic year."

100,000 Light-years Wide
4×10⁶ M☉ Central Black Hole
26,000 ly To Galactic Center
🌠

Andromeda Galaxy

Our nearest major galactic neighbor is approaching us at 250,000 mph and will collide with the Milky Way in about 4.5 billion years, creating a new galaxy astronomers have nicknamed "Milkomeda."

2.5M ly Distance
1T Stars
4.5B years Until Collision
🕳️

Sagittarius A*

The supermassive black hole at our galaxy's center has a mass 4 million times that of our Sun. In 2022, the Event Horizon Telescope captured the first image of its shadow, confirming Einstein's predictions.

4M M☉ Mass
24M km Event Horizon
7,000 km/s Orbital Velocity

Exotic Phenomena

The universe's most mysterious and extreme manifestations of physics

🌑

Dark Matter

This invisible substance makes up 85% of all matter in the universe. Though we can't see it directly, its gravitational effects shape the large-scale structure of the cosmos and hold galaxies together.

27% Of Universe
5:1 vs Normal Matter
Unknown Composition
⚡

Dark Energy

The mysterious force driving the accelerating expansion of the universe. Discovered in 1998, dark energy comprises about 68% of the total energy density of the observable universe.

68% Of Universe
1998 Discovery Year
Repulsive Gravity Effect
🌊

Gravitational Waves

Ripples in spacetime itself, predicted by Einstein and first detected in 2015. These waves travel at light speed and are created by the most violent events in the universe, like colliding black holes.

2015 First Detection
10⁻²¹ Strain Amplitude
c Speed
