The Universe Around Us

🌌 Our Galaxy – The Milky Way

The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy spanning roughly 100 000 light-years. Home to more than 100 billion stars, it hosts giant clouds of gas, dust, ancient clusters and a super-massive black hole at its centre. Our Solar System orbits this centre at ≈220 km/s, taking 230 million years per revolution.

☀️ Our Solar System

Formed 4.6 billion years ago, the Solar System is bound to the Sun by gravity. It contains eight planets, 200+ moons, dwarf planets, asteroids and comets. 99.8 % of the system’s mass lies in the Sun, a G-type star fusing hydrogen into helium. Rocky inner worlds contrast with the gas and ice giants beyond the Asteroid Belt.

🌍 Planet Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known world with life. Vast oceans cover 71 % of its surface, while a magnetic field and atmosphere shield life from radiation. Plate tectonics sculpt continents and recycle nutrients; the Moon stabilises Earth’s tilt, producing tides that nurture ecosystems.
