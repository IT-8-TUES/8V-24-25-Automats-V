History of Space Exploration

Important Dates and Events

October 4, 1957: USSR launches the first artificial satellite – Sputnik 1.

November 3, 1957: Laika becomes the first animal in orbit aboard Sputnik 2.

April 12, 1961: Yuri Gagarin becomes the first human in space.

June 16, 1963: Valentina Tereshkova becomes the first woman in space.

March 18, 1965: Alexei Leonov performs the first spacewalk.

July 20, 1969: Apollo 11 lands on the Moon – Neil Armstrong is the first person to walk on the Moon.

April 19, 1971: Salyut 1 – the first space station.

1975: Apollo-Soyuz Test Project – the first international space mission (USA & USSR).

1981: USA launches the first reusable space shuttle – Columbia.

1990: Launch of the Hubble Space Telescope.

November 20, 1998: Construction of the International Space Station (ISS) begins.

2004: SpaceShipOne becomes the first private manned spacecraft to reach space.

2012: NASA’s Curiosity rover lands on Mars.

2015: New Horizons spacecraft flies by Pluto.

2020: NASA's Perseverance rover launches to Mars.

2021: First spaceflights with tourists – Blue Origin and SpaceX.

2023: India’s Chandrayaan-3 mission lands near the Moon’s south pole.

Black Holes

A black hole is a region in space where gravity is so strong that not even light can escape.

It forms when a very massive star collapses under its own gravity at the end of its life.

The first black hole ever discovered was Cygnus X-1 in 1964.

White Holes

White holes are theoretical opposites of black holes. While nothing can escape a black hole,

nothing can enter a white hole. They are not proven to exist, but are predicted by some solutions to Einstein's equations.

Stars

Stars are giant balls of hot gas, mainly hydrogen, that shine by nuclear fusion.

They go through stages: formation, main sequence, red giant, and finally either white dwarf,

neutron star, or black hole depending on their mass. Our Sun is a medium-sized star.

Planets

Mercury

Mercury has been known since ancient times. It was first visited by the Mariner 10 spacecraft in 1974.

Venus

Venus is very bright in the night sky. The Soviet Venera missions gave us the first close-up images in the 1970s.

Earth

Earth is our home and the only known planet to support life.

Mars

Mars has been observed for centuries. NASA's Viking 1 was the first spacecraft to land there in 1976.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet. It was studied by Galileo in 1610, who saw its four biggest moons.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its rings. The Cassini mission explored it from 2004 to 2017.

Uranus

Uranus was discovered by William Herschel in 1781 using a telescope.

Neptune

Neptune was predicted with math before it was seen. It was discovered in 1846.

Pluto

Pluto was discovered in 1930 and was considered the ninth planet until it was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006.
